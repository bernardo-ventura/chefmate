# NUTRITION GUIDE - Meal Planner

## Healthy Eating Guidelines

### What Makes a Meal Healthy?

A healthy meal should:
- **Calories**: Between 300-600 calories per meal
- **Balance**: Include protein, carbs, and vegetables
- **Quality**: Prioritize whole, unprocessed foods
- **Portions**: Appropriate serving sizes

---

## Calorie Ranges by Meal Type

- **Breakfast**: 300-400 calories
- **Lunch**: 400-600 calories
- **Dinner**: 400-600 calories
- **Snacks**: 100-200 calories

## Sample Healthy Meals

### 🥗 Salada Caesar (350 cal)
- 100g lettuce (15 cal)
- 150g grilled chicken (248 cal)
- 20g parmesan (78 cal)
- 10ml olive oil dressing (88 cal)
- **Total**: ~350 calories
- **Is Healthy**: Yes ✓

### 🍚 Arroz com Feijão (450 cal)
- 200g brown rice (222 cal)
- 100g black beans (132 cal)
- 100g grilled chicken (165 cal)
- Vegetables (negligible)
- **Total**: ~450 calories
- **Is Healthy**: Yes ✓

### 🍝 Pasta with Vegetables (380 cal)
- 150g whole wheat pasta (186 cal)
- 200g mixed vegetables (80 cal)
- 100g tomato sauce (40 cal)
- 50g grilled chicken (83 cal)
- **Total**: ~380 calories
- **Is Healthy**: Yes ✓

### 🍕 Pizza Margherita (800 cal)
- Dough, cheese, tomato sauce

- **Is Healthy**: No ✗ (too many calories, processed)

## Meal Planning Tips

1. **Prep Ingredients First**
   - Wash and cut vegetables
   - Cook proteins in batches
   - Portion carbs (use a scale!)

2. **Use Proper Quantities**
   - Format: "200g", "1 xícara", "150ml"
   - Be specific for accurate tracking

3. **Balance Your Plate**
   - 50% vegetables
   - 25% protein
   - 25% complex carbs

4. **Track Calories**
   - Use the system to calculate totals
   - Adjust portions as needed
   - Aim for 1500-2000 cal/day (varies by person)

---

## Warning Signs of Unhealthy Meals

❌ **Too Many Calories**: > 700 per meal
❌ **Processed Foods**: Fast food, frozen meals
❌ **Too Much Fat**: Fried foods, heavy sauces
❌ **Low Nutrients**: Mostly carbs, no vegetables
❌ **Large Portions**: More than needed

---

## Common Ingredient Quantities

- **Rice**: 200g cooked = 1 cup
- **Chicken**: 150g = palm of hand
- **Vegetables**: 200g = 2 cups
- **Oil**: 1 tablespoon = 15ml
- **Beans**: 100g = 1/2 cup

---

## Hydration

Don't forget water!
- **Recommendation**: 2-3 liters per day
- Drink before meals to help with portion control
- Avoid sugary drinks

---

## Questions to Ask When Planning

1. Does this meal have protein?
2. Does it include vegetables?
3. Are the carbs whole/complex?
4. Is the total under 600 calories?
5. Will I feel satisfied after eating?

If you answered YES to most questions, it's probably healthy! ✓
